Den


About the game

DEN is an interactive diorama populated by social creatures.
The only way to interact with them is by moving your cursor in the scene.
It was made to question the observer's neutrality, and sensitize the impact on an environnement.

Note : This is more of an experience than a game. The interaction is limited, and the focus is more set on observation and patience.

My Contributions

I was a solo game programmer on this project, I worked with a Game Designer, a Sound Designer, a UX researcher and two Game Artists.

The AI of the creatures was a main challenge, which we did overcome by collaborating a lot with the Game Designer and asked for advises by Axel Buendia, researcher in AI and director of the Cnam ENJMIN.

For the AI I used a method called "Smart Objects", where the AIs have simple wants and the objects contain the majority of the logic needed.
A flower knows how to be eaten and tells the AIs how much they want to eat it for example.

I also took some time with the UX researcher to add accessibility features such as close captioning and an "Automatic Mode" (ressembling a god mode to easily experiment with the AIs), those features were important to me, made me discover accessibility fields I did not knew before and helped me debug.
